The header bar sits at the top of the main window and gives you context about where you are and quick access to global controls. Its contents change depending on the active view—each view declares which header buttons should appear through a UI configuration—but the bar always anchors navigation and project identity at the top of the screen.

In most editing views the header shows a **Back** button, a title, and the song avatar. The title is static on some screens (for example "Projects" or "Mixer") and dynamic on others: in Clip Detail it shows the current track or instrument name, and in the launcher or arrangement it reflects your current selection. Tapping the song avatar opens Settings. A BPM readout appears in views where tempo is relevant, and a **View Visibility** control lets you show or hide optional panels such as the scene launcher or pattern dock.

When you are inside a project, the back button usually returns to the previous view; from the Clip Launcher or Arrangement it switches to a project-library icon and takes you back to the Projects screen. An **Arrangement** toggle switches between the Clip Launcher and the Arrangement view, choosing horizontal or vertical layout based on device orientation. On Android, the header may hide in fullscreen mode to maximize editing space.
