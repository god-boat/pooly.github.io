# Clips, Patterns, and Scenes

These three ideas are central to how Pooly organizes music.

## Clips

A **clip** is a container of musical data — notes, automation, or audio — that lives in a slot on a track. You can think of a clip as one loop or section of a part.

## Patterns

A **pattern** holds the note data inside a clip: which notes play, when they start, how long they last, and how hard they're hit (velocity). When you open the Clip Detail view to draw notes on the piano roll, you're editing a pattern.

## Scenes

A **scene** is a horizontal row across all tracks in the Clip Launcher. Launching a scene triggers every clip in that row at once, which makes it easy to switch between sections of a song — like jumping from a verse to a chorus.

## How they fit together

```
Scene 1  ┃  Clip A (Track 1)  │  Clip B (Track 2)  │  Clip C (Track 3)
Scene 2  ┃  Clip D (Track 1)  │  Clip E (Track 2)  │  Clip F (Track 3)
```

Each clip contains a pattern. Scenes let you launch groups of clips together. Tracks run vertically and each one has its own instrument and effects chain.
